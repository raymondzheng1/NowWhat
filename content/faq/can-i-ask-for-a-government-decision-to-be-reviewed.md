Many decisions made by government agencies can be looked at again if you disagree with
them. Whether yours can, and how, depends on the law the decision was made under.

## What review usually looks like

There are often two steps. First, you can ask the agency that made the decision to look
at it again. If you still disagree, you may be able to ask an independent tribunal to
review it. Many of these reviews are free.

## Time limits

There is usually a time limit to ask for a review, and it can be short. Finding the date
early keeps your options open.

## Where to get free help

A free legal service can confirm whether your decision can be reviewed, by when, and
what the steps are for your own situation.
