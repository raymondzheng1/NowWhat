## What counts as special circumstances?

Special circumstances means that at the time you got the fine, one of these things was true for you:

- You had a **mental illness or disability**
- You had a **serious addiction**
- You were **homeless**
- You were affected by **family violence**

The situation also needs to have affected your behaviour at the time.

## What evidence might help?

You will generally need to show something that backs up your claim. Useful documents can include:

- A **letter or report from a doctor, caseworker, or support service** that relates to your situation
- The **original fine or notice**, including its infringement number
- Any **letters showing what stage your fine has reached**

Acting early is important — what you can do depends on the stage your fine has reached, so it helps to start before your fine becomes a **Notice of Final Demand**.

## Who reviews a special circumstances claim?

You apply to the agency that issued the fine, or to **Fines Victoria**. After a review, the agency must tell you the outcome in writing.

Note: you can only ask for **one review per fine**.

## Where can I get free help?

You do not have to deal with this alone. Free help is available:

- **Fines Victoria** — (03) 9200 8111
- **Victoria Legal Aid** — Legal Help line 1300 792 387
- **Victorian Aboriginal Legal Service (VALS)** — 1800 064 865 (for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people)
- **National Debt Helpline** — 1800 007 007 (free financial counselling)
