## What the law requires

A notice to vacate in Victoria must meet three things:

- It must be on the **correct, up-to-date form**.
- It must give a **valid legal reason** for asking you to leave.
- It must give a **notice period that matches the reason** — if the period is too short, the notice may not be valid.

If any of these things are wrong or missing, the notice may not hold up.

## Reasons a notice might not be valid

Your notice may have a problem if:

- It uses the wrong form or an out-of-date form.
- It is missing required information.
- The notice period is shorter than the law allows for that reason.
- The reason on the notice is not a lawful reason.
- The reason is not genuine — for example, it says "sale" but there is no real plan to sell.
- You were given the notice because you asked for repairs or stood up for your rights.

## What to check straight away

Gather these things and look them over carefully:

- The notice itself — check the date, the form used, and the reason given.
- Your rental agreement (lease) and any condition report.
- Your rent payment records.
- Any messages with your rental provider or agent, including when you got the notice.
- Photos of the condition of the place.
- The envelope or postmark showing when the notice arrived.

## How to get free help

There are time limits involved, so it helps to act early. You can [start by finding out about your options here](/start).

Free help is available from:

- **Tenants Victoria** — Rental Support Line **(03) 9416 2577**
- **Rental Dispute Resolution Victoria (RDRV)** — **(1300 017 378)**
- **Victoria Legal Aid** — Legal Help line **(1300 792 387)**
- **Consumer Affairs Victoria** — free renting information

You can also apply to **VCAT** to challenge a notice to vacate. If you miss the early deadline, you may still be able to argue the notice is not valid at a possession hearing, or ask for more time.
