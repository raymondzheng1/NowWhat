## What can you do if you disagree with a Centrelink debt?

You have the right to ask Services Australia to review most Centrelink decisions, including debts. This is called an **internal review**. An **Authorised Review Officer (ARO)** — someone inside Services Australia but separate from the original decision-maker — will look at it again.

You can also ask Services Australia to give you a **written statement of reasons** explaining why the debt was raised.

If you still disagree after the internal review, you can usually apply to the **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)**. The ART is independent of Centrelink.

**Time limits can apply to some steps.** Acting early is important, because delays can affect your options and any back-payment you might be owed. Check the current time limits before you act.

## How do you start?

1. **Contact Services Australia** and say you disagree with the debt. Ask for the decision to be reviewed by an Authorised Review Officer.
2. **Gather your documents**, such as:
   - The debt notice or decision letter (including any reference number)
   - Payslips, bank statements, or records showing your income and dates
   - Any letters or messages you have received from Services Australia
3. **Apply to the ART** if you are still unhappy after the internal review.

## What reasons can you give for disputing the debt?

You can dispute a debt if you think:
- The facts used (such as income figures or dates) were wrong
- The debt amount was worked out incorrectly
- The rules were not applied correctly to your situation
- Your personal circumstances were not properly taken into account

## Where can you get free help?

- **Economic Justice Australia (welfare rights centres)** — free, independent help with Centrelink decisions and debts
- **National Debt Helpline** — free, independent financial counselling on 1800 007 007
- **Services Australia** — you can ask them directly about the review process
- **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** — for independent external review

Free welfare rights services can explain your options without charging you.
