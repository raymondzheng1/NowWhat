## What is the ART?

The ART is independent. It is not part of Centrelink. It can look at many Centrelink decisions again and decide if they were right.

## What steps come before the ART?

Before you go to the ART, you usually need to ask Services Australia to review the decision first. This is called an **internal review**. An Authorised Review Officer (ARO) — someone inside Services Australia — looks at the decision again.

If you still disagree after the internal review, you can then apply to the ART.

## What reasons can you challenge a decision on?

You can ask for a review if you think any of these apply:

- The decision got the **facts wrong** — for example, the income or dates used
- A **debt was worked out incorrectly**
- The **rules were applied incorrectly** to your situation
- Your **circumstances were not properly taken into account**

## What should you gather before applying?

Having the right paperwork can help. Useful documents include:

- The **decision letter or debt notice**, including any reference number
- **Payslips, bank statements or records** showing your income and dates
- **Letters from a doctor, caseworker or support service**, if relevant
- Any **letters or messages** you have had from Services Australia about the decision

You can also ask Services Australia for a **written statement of reasons** explaining why they made the decision.

## Are there time limits?

Yes — time limits can apply to applying to the ART. Missing a deadline can limit your options. The exact time limit depends on your decision. It is important to check it before you apply.

Acting early can also matter if you want any back-payment. This includes the internal review stage.

## Where can you get free help?

Free services are available to help you understand your options:

- **Economic Justice Australia** (welfare rights centres) — free, independent help with Centrelink decisions and debts
- **National Debt Helpline** — free, independent financial counselling on 1800 007 007
- **Services Australia** — you can ask them to explain or review the decision
- **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** — for external review after the internal review
