## What does 'mistake of identity' mean?

If you received a fine but someone else was actually responsible, this is called a **mistake of identity**. It is one of the official grounds for asking for a review of your fine in Victoria.

You are saying: "I was not the person who should have received this fine."

## What do you need to do?

You ask for a **review** of your fine. You choose the ground that says you were not the person responsible.

You will need to provide **proof** that you were not the driver or owner at the time. For example, this could be a document or record showing someone else was using the vehicle.

You can only ask for **one review per fine**, so it helps to gather your evidence before you apply.

## When should you act?

You can generally ask for a review **before your fine is registered** — that is, before you receive a 'Notice of Final Demand'. Acting early gives you more choices. Once a fine reaches the Notice of Final Demand stage, your options are more limited.

For excessive-speed, drink-driving, or drug-driving offences, different deadlines apply — ask for help early to make sure you do not miss them.

## Where can you get free help?

You do not have to handle this alone. Free help is available:

- **Fines Victoria** — manage, review or get help with a Victorian fine: **(03) 9200 8111**
- **Victoria Legal Aid** — free legal help with fines: **1300 792 387**
- **Victorian Aboriginal Legal Service (VALS)** — free help for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people: **1800 064 865**
- **National Debt Helpline** — free financial counselling: **1800 007 007**
