## What is an internal review?

An internal review means asking Services Australia to look at their decision again. A person called an **Authorised Review Officer (ARO)** checks it. This person was not part of the first decision.

You can ask for an internal review if you think:

- The decision got the facts wrong (for example, the wrong income or dates were used)
- A debt was worked out incorrectly
- The rules were not applied correctly to your situation
- Your circumstances were not properly taken into account

You can also ask Services Australia for a **written statement of reasons** explaining why the decision was made.

## What if I still disagree after the internal review?

If you are still unhappy, you can usually apply to the **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)**. The ART is independent. It is not part of Centrelink.

Time limits can apply to some steps in this process. Acting early can also matter for any back-payment you may be owed. Confirm any current time limits before you act.

## What information helps with a review?

Gathering documents can support your review. Useful things to have include:

- The decision letter or debt notice, including any reference number
- Payslips, bank statements or records showing your income and dates
- Letters from a doctor, caseworker or support service, if relevant
- Any letters or messages you have had from Services Australia about the decision

## Where can I get free help?

Free, independent services can explain your options without charging you:

- **Economic Justice Australia** (welfare rights centres) — free help with Centrelink decisions and debts
- **National Debt Helpline** — free financial counselling on 1800 007 007
