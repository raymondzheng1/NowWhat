## What happened and why

When Centrelink cancels a payment, they must explain why. You can ask Services Australia for a **written statement of reasons**. This can help you see what went wrong. For example, they may have used the wrong income figures or wrong dates, or missed your circumstances.

Having that written explanation can also help you decide what to do next.

## How to challenge the cancellation

There are two main steps you can take.

**Step 1 — Internal review**
Tell Services Australia you disagree. Ask for the decision to be reviewed by an **Authorised Review Officer (ARO)**. This is a review done inside Services Australia. The officer was not part of the first decision.

Asking sooner can matter for any back-payment you might be owed if the decision is changed. A time limit may apply to some steps — confirm the current rule for your decision before you act.

**Step 2 — Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)**
If you still disagree, you can usually apply to the **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)**. The ART is independent of Centrelink. There is a time limit to apply for many decisions. Check the current one before you apply.

You can [start by learning more about your options here](/start).

## What to gather

Having the right documents can help your review. Useful things to collect include:

- The cancellation letter, including any reference number
- Payslips, bank statements, or records showing your income and relevant dates
- Letters from a doctor, caseworker, or support service, if relevant
- Any previous letters or messages from Services Australia about your payment

## Where to get free help

You do not have to do this alone. Free, independent help is available:

- **Economic Justice Australia (welfare rights centres)** — free help with Centrelink decisions
- **National Debt Helpline** — free financial counselling on 1800 007 007
- **Services Australia** — you can ask them directly about the reviews process
- **Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)** — handles independent external reviews

Free services can explain what the decision means and what your options are without charging you anything.
