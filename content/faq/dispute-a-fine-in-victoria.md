If you got a fine or infringement notice in Victoria and you disagree with it, or you
can't pay, you have options. Which ones you can use depends on the stage your fine has
reached.

## The stages

A fine moves through stages. First an Infringement Notice, then a Penalty Reminder
Notice, then a Notice of Final Demand, then enforcement. Some options, like going to
court or nominating another driver, only work before the fine is registered. That is
before the Notice of Final Demand. So acting early keeps more options open.

## Ask for a review

You can ask for a review on one of five grounds. These are: you did not know about the
fine; the decision was against the law; it was a case of mistaken identity; there were
exceptional circumstances; or there were special circumstances. Special circumstances
include a mental illness or disability, a serious addiction, homelessness, or family
violence. If you only just found out about the fine, ask within 14 days of finding out.

## Other options

You can nominate the person who was actually responsible, ask to have the matter heard in
court, or ask Fines Victoria for a payment plan or a Work and Development Permit if you
are in hardship.

## Get free help

Victoria Legal Aid's Legal Help line is 1300 792 387. The National Debt Helpline gives
free financial counselling on 1800 007 007.
