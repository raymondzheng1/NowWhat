---
id: merits-review
name: "Merits review"
plainName: "Asking for a fresh look at the decision"
oneLine: "A tribunal looks at your case again and can make a different decision."
question: "Is this the correct or preferable decision?"
whatItIs: "Merits review means an independent tribunal looks at the decision again — on the facts and the law — as if it were being made fresh. The tribunal stands in the shoes of the original decision-maker and applies the same rules. It can agree with the decision, change part of it, or replace it with a different one. It is usually cheaper, faster, and less formal than going to court."
bodies:
  - { jurisdiction: Vic, name: "VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal)", note: "Hears many Victorian decisions where an Act gives a right of review." }
  - { jurisdiction: Cth, name: "ART (Administrative Review Tribunal)", note: "Hears many Australian Government decisions, such as Centrelink." }
canApply:
  - "There must be a law that gives a right of review for this kind of decision."
  - "You usually need to be a person affected by the decision (this is called standing)."
  - "There is almost always a time limit — this is the part people miss most."
whatHappens:
  - "You lodge an application with the tribunal."
  - "Often there is an informal stage that tries to sort it out first."
  - "If it goes to a hearing, you explain your side and give your evidence."
  - "The tribunal makes a fresh decision."
remedies:
  - "Affirm — keep the decision the same."
  - "Vary — change part of the decision."
  - "Set aside and substitute — replace it with a new decision."
limits:
  - "Merits review is only available where a law provides it — not every decision can be reviewed on the merits."
goodToKnow:
  - "Because the tribunal can change the outcome, merits review is usually what people want when they disagree with the result."
sources:
  - "VCAT — vcat.vic.gov.au"
  - "Administrative Review Tribunal — art.gov.au"
