id: judicial-review
name: "Judicial review"
plainName: "Asking a court to check the decision was made lawfully"
oneLine: "A court checks whether the decision was made the right way — not whether it was a good decision."
question: "Was the decision made lawfully?"
whatItIs: "Judicial review is different from merits review. A court does not ask whether the decision was right or fair — it asks whether it was made lawfully: did the decision-maker have the power, follow a fair process, and stay within the law? If the court finds a legal error — a 'ground of review' — it can set the decision aside. But the court cannot make a new decision for you. It usually sends the matter back to be decided again, properly."

  - { jurisdiction: Vic, name: "Supreme Court of Victoria (Order 56)", note: "Judicial review of Victorian government decisions." }
  - { jurisdiction: Cth, name: "Federal Court / Federal Circuit and Family Court", note: "Judicial review of Australian Government decisions (ADJR Act; s 39B Judiciary Act)." }
  - { jurisdiction: Cth, name: "High Court (s 75(v) Constitution)", note: "Constitutional judicial review of Commonwealth officers." }
canApply:
  - "You need a 'ground of review' — a specific legal error in how the decision was made."
  - "You usually need to be a person affected by the decision (standing)."
  - "There are strict time limits, and they are usually NOT paused by asking for reasons."
whatHappens:
  - "You start a proceeding in the court."
  - "You argue that one or more grounds of review apply."
  - "The court looks at the legality of the decision, not its merits."
  - "If a ground is made out, the court can set the decision aside."
remedies:
  - "Set aside (quash) the decision."
  - "Send it back (remit) to be decided again, lawfully."
  - "Orders to require or stop action, in some cases."
limits:
  - "The court cannot substitute its own decision — it cannot simply give you the outcome you want, only require a lawful re-decision."
  - "Judicial review is about legality, not about whether the decision was harsh or wrong on the facts."
goodToKnow:
  - "Judicial review can be technical and the time limits are tight — getting free legal help early really matters."
