---
id: error-of-law
name: Error of law
plainName: They got the law wrong
oneLine: >-
  The decision-maker applied the wrong legal test, or misread what the law
  requires.
whatItMeans: >-
  A decision can be challenged where the decision-maker made a legal error — for
  example, applying the wrong test, misreading what a law requires, or
  misunderstanding a legal word. Some legal errors are reviewable even when they
  appear on the face of the written decision.
plainExample: A decision applies a stricter test than the law actually sets.
whatRelates:
  - The decision seems to apply the wrong rule or test.
  - The written reasons misstate what the law requires.
  - A legal word or requirement looks like it was misunderstood.
whatItIsNot: >-
  It does not cover a disagreement about the facts — it is about the law being
  applied wrongly.
usedIn:
  - judicial-review
test: An error of law (including one on the face of the record) may be reviewable.

  - id: wrong-test

    layPrompt: Did they use the wrong legal test or standard?
  - id: misread-law
    name: Misreading the law
    layPrompt: Do the reasons misstate what the law requires?
