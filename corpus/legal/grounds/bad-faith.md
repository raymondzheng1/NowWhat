---
id: bad-faith
name: Bad faith
plainName: The decision was not made honestly
oneLine: 'The decision was made dishonestly, or for a deliberately improper reason.'
whatItMeans: >-
  A decision made dishonestly, or for a deliberately improper reason, can be
  unlawful. Bad faith is rare and hard to prove, and it is a serious allegation
  — it means much more than a mistake or even an unfair process.
plainExample: A decision is made to deliberately harm someone for personal reasons.
whatRelates:
  - There are real signs the decision was not made honestly.
  - The decision looks deliberately aimed at harming you for an improper reason.
whatItIsNot: >-
  It does not cover an honest mistake or an unfair process — those are other
  grounds. Bad faith is a high bar and a serious claim.
usedIn:
  - judicial-review
test: >-
  A decision made in bad faith may be invalid; it is a serious allegation that
  needs strong material.
elements:
  - id: dishonesty
    name: Dishonesty or deliberate impropriety
    layPrompt: Are there real signs the decision was not made honestly?
