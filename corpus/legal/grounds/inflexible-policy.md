---
id: inflexible-policy
name: Inflexible policy / acting under dictation
plainName: They followed a rule or an order instead of deciding your case
oneLine: >-
  A discretion was applied so rigidly, or so much at someone else's direction,
  that your case wasn't really considered.
whatItMeans: >-
  A decision-maker who has a discretion must actually use it. Applying a policy
  so rigidly that they do not consider your particular circumstances, or simply
  doing what another person or body directed, can be unlawful.
plainExample: >-
  An application is refused 'because that is our policy', without anyone looking
  at your specific situation.
whatRelates:
  - 'You were told ''that is just our policy'', with no look at your circumstances.'
  - The decision-maker seemed to be following someone else's direction.
  - Your particular situation does not seem to have been considered at all.
whatItIsNot: >-
  It does not mean policies are not allowed — only that they cannot be applied
  with no room for your case.
usedIn:
  - judicial-review
test: >-
  Fettering a discretion by an inflexible policy, or acting under another's
  dictation, may make a decision invalid.
elements:
  - id: rigid-policy
    name: Inflexible application of policy
    layPrompt: Was a policy applied without looking at your circumstances?
  - id: dictation
    name: Acting under dictation
    layPrompt: Did the decision-maker just do what someone else told them to?
sources:
  - Victoria Legal Aid — legalaid.vic.gov.au
status: verified
