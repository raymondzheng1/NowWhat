---
id: relevant-considerations
name: Relevant and irrelevant considerations
plainName: >-
  They ignored something they had to weigh, or relied on something they
  shouldn't
oneLine: >-
  A decision-maker must weigh what the law says matters — and ignore what it
  says doesn't.
whatItMeans: >-
  A decision-maker must take into account the things the law requires them to
  consider, and must not rely on things the law says are irrelevant. Leaving out
  a required consideration, or relying on a forbidden one, can make a decision
  unlawful.
plainExample: >-
  A housing decision ignores the medical evidence that the rules say must be
  taken into account.
whatRelates:
  - Something the rules say must be considered appears to have been left out.
  - The decision relied on something that should not have mattered.
  - >-
    You gave important information that the decision-maker did not appear to
    weigh.
whatItIsNot: >-
  It does not mean every point you raised had to be accepted — only that the
  required matters had to be genuinely considered.
usedIn:
  - judicial-review
test: >-
  A decision-maker must consider the matters the law requires, and must not rely
  on matters the law forbids.
elements:
  - id: ignored-relevant
    name: Ignored a required consideration
    layPrompt: >-
      Was there something important the law required them to take into account
      that they seem to have ignored?
  - id: took-irrelevant
    name: Relied on an irrelevant consideration
    layPrompt: Did they rely on something that shouldn't have mattered to this decision?
sources:
