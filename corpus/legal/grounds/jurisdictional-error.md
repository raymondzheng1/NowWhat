id: jurisdictional-error
name: Jurisdictional error
plainName: The decision-maker went beyond their power
oneLine: 'The decision-maker acted outside their power, or asked the wrong question.'
whatItMeans: >-
  A decision can be unlawful if the decision-maker acted beyond the power the
  law gives them — for example by asking the wrong question, ignoring a step the
  law required first, or misunderstanding the limits of their role. This is a
  central idea in judicial review, especially for Commonwealth decisions.
plainExample: A body decides something only a different body had the power to decide.
whatRelates:
  - The decision-maker may not have had the power to make this decision.
  - A step the law required first seems to have been skipped.
  - >-
    The decision-maker seems to have misunderstood what they were allowed to
    decide.
whatItIsNot: >-
  It does not mean any mistake — it has to be an error that goes to the
  decision-maker's power.
usedIn:
  - judicial-review
test: >-
  An error going to the existence or exercise of power (a jurisdictional error)
  may make a decision invalid.

  - id: wrong-question
    name: Asking the wrong question
    layPrompt: 'Did they decide on the wrong question, or ignore a required step?'
